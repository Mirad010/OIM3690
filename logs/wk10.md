# Week 10 Log

## What I worked on

Fixed the biggest problem from last week: the map looked interactive but wasn't connected to the calculations. You could click a different city and the currency/pin would change, but the financial numbers stayed the same, which defeats the point of a "feasibility" tool.

Didn't want to fake city-specific numbers, so I looked for real data instead. Ended up combining two APIs: World Bank GDP per capita by country, to scale construction cost and room rate to the local economy, and OpenStreetMap's Overpass API, to count real hotels/attractions/restaurants within 3 km of the clicked pin and estimate occupancy from that.

Also went back and fixed my documentation — my proposal text was sitting in README.md by mistake instead of a separate PROPOSAL.md. Split those properly, added the "what I don't know yet" section I'd skipped the first time, and wrote WORKLOG.md with weekly entries.

## What I learned

Free public APIs fail in ways you don't expect — the Overpass request returned nothing the first time I tested it in class, not because the idea was wrong but because it was sent as a POST when it needed to be GET, and the main server is sometimes too busy to answer at all. Added two backup mirror servers so it doesn't just break silently.

## AI interactions

Worked through the map problem with AI — tried a couple of approaches before landing on World Bank + OpenStreetMap, since the obvious idea (real construction cost by exact address) doesn't have a free API. Used it again to debug the Overpass request once it started failing, and to practice explaining the map-to-sliders connection out loud before Demo Day, since I didn't fully understand that part myself the first time I looked back at my own code.
